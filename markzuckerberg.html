Mark Zuckerberg

Mark Elliot Zuckerberg, born on May 14, 1984, in White Plains, New York, is one of the most prominent figures in the digital age. Raised in nearby Dobbs Ferry, he grew up in a well-educated and supportive family. His father, Edward Zuckerberg, is a dentist, and his mother, Karen Kempner, is a psychiatrist. Mark was the only boy among four siblings and was known for his intelligence and curiosity from an early age. Zuckerberg’s interest in computers began in middle school. By the age of 12, he had created a messaging program called "ZuckNet" for his father’s dental practice. This early creation hinted at his future potential in software development. His parents recognized his talent and hired a private computer tutor. In high school at Phillips Exeter Academy, Zuckerberg excelled academically and continued developing software, including a music player called Synapse, which attracted attention from companies like Microsoft and AOL. He, however, turned down their offers and instead chose to attend Harvard University in 2002. At Harvard, Zuckerberg initially focused on psychology and computer science. During his sophomore year, he launched a website called "Facemash", a controversial site that allowed students to rate their peers' attractiveness. Though the site was quickly shut down by the university, it sparked the idea for what would become Facebook. In February 2004, Mark and his college roommates — Eduardo Saverin, Dustin Moskovitz, Andrew McCollum, and Chris Hughes — launched TheFacebook.com, initially limited to Harvard students. The site quickly spread to other Ivy League universities and then to colleges across the United States. Recognizing the potential of the platform, Zuckerberg dropped out of Harvard in 2004 and moved to Palo Alto, California, to focus on Facebook full-time. Over the years, the platform evolved into Facebook, the world’s largest social networking site. Zuckerberg’s vision was clear: to connect people globally. Under his leadership, Facebook introduced features such as the News Feed, Pages, Messenger, and more. In 2012, Facebook went public, making Zuckerberg one of the youngest self-made billionaires at the age of 28. Mark's success didn't stop with Facebook. He went on to acquire several companies including Instagram (2012), WhatsApp (2014), and Oculus VR (2014), expanding Facebook's reach into different areas of technology and communication. In 2021, Zuckerberg rebranded Facebook’s parent company to Meta Platforms Inc., reflecting his ambitions to build the metaverse — a virtual shared space blending augmented and virtual reality. Despite controversies over privacy, misinformation, and the platform’s influence on society, Zuckerberg remains a central figure in the tech industry. He has also committed to philanthropy through the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, co-founded with his wife Priscilla Chan, pledging to donate a majority of their wealth to causes related to education, science, and equality. From a precocious child fascinated by coding to a global tech leader shaping the future of the internet, Mark Zuckerberg’s journey is a compelling story of vision, innovation, and relentless drive.
