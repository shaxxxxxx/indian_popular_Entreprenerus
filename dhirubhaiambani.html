Dhirubhai Ambani

Dhirajlal Hirachand Ambani, popularly known as Dhirubhai Ambani, was born on December 28, 1932, in the small village of Chorwad, in the Junagadh district of Gujarat, India. He was born into a modest family; his father, Hirachand Gordhanbhai Ambani, was a schoolteacher, and his mother, Jamnaben, was a homemaker. Growing up in a household with limited means, Dhirubhai learned the values of thrift, hard work, and ambition early in life. Due to the family’s financial constraints, Dhirubhai’s formal education was limited. He completed his schooling up to matriculation (10th grade). Although he could not pursue a college education, his sharp mind, street-smart attitude, and hunger to succeed proved to be far more valuable in the long run. At the age of 17, Dhirubhai left for Aden (Yemen), then a British colony, where he worked as a clerk for A. Besse & Co., a trading firm dealing in petroleum products. During his years in Aden, he gained a solid understanding of trade, shipping, currency arbitrage, and international markets. This practical exposure laid the foundation for his entrepreneurial ambitions. In 1958, Dhirubhai returned to India with modest savings and set up a small business in Mumbai (then Bombay). Along with his cousin Champaklal Damani, he started an import-export business named Reliance Commercial Corporation, trading in polyester yarn and spices. The business grew steadily, and by the 1960s, Dhirubhai began venturing into textile manufacturing. In 1966, he established a textile mill in Naroda, Gujarat, and soon launched the brand “Vimal”, which became synonymous with high-quality and affordable fabric in India. His success was not just in manufacturing but also in branding and retailing. He revolutionized the textile industry by building a nationwide retail network and introducing polyester to Indian consumers. The turning point came in the 1970s and 1980s when Dhirubhai took the bold step of entering the Indian stock market. He listed Reliance Industries on the Bombay Stock Exchange in 1977, making it one of the first Indian companies to attract ordinary investors in large numbers. His ability to build trust among small investors and reward them with handsome returns became legendary. Under his dynamic leadership, Reliance Industries diversified beyond textiles into petrochemicals, refining, telecommunications, energy, and infrastructure. His vision was clear: to build a self-reliant India through world-class industries. Despite facing criticism, political opposition, and regulatory hurdles, he persisted with courage and strategic insight. By the late 1990s, Reliance had become India’s largest private-sector enterprise, and Dhirubhai was widely admired for turning a small trading company into a corporate powerhouse. He was honored with numerous awards and was posthumously awarded the Padma Vibhushan in 2016 for his contribution to Indian industry. Dhirubhai Ambani passed away on July 6, 2002, at the age of 69. After his death, the Reliance empire was split between his two sons, Mukesh Ambani and Anil Ambani. Mukesh continues to lead Reliance Industries Limited (RIL), which remains a pillar of the Indian economy. Dhirubhai’s journey from a rural village boy with little formal education to one of India’s most iconic industrialists is a story of vision, grit, and relentless ambition. He famously said, “Think big, think fast, think ahead. Ideas are no one’s monopoly.” His life is a powerful reminder that determination and entrepreneurship can break through any barrier.
